## 1. The Biz

1.1. **Activities:** Oklo develops advanced fission power plants using liquid metal fast reactor technology. It plans to commercialize the Aurora powerhouse, designed to produce up to 15 MWe on recycled or fresh nuclear fuel. The company pursues two tracks: selling reliable commercial-scale energy and offering used nuclear fuel recycling services in the U.S.

1.2. **Profitable?:** No — it is pre-revenue with cumulative net losses (~$74.5M) and negative operating/free cash flow; management aims to deploy its first commercial reactor by 2027 per news, implying profitability only after commercialization begins.

1.3. **Customer & Markets:** Primary customers are commercial and industrial energy users needing reliable, on-site power (including AI/data centers and remote power needs cited in news). Markets are focused on the United States, including U.S. customers for used nuclear fuel recycling.

1.4. **Competition:** Key competitor is NuScale Power (SMR) in small modular/microreactors, and investor comparisons include Cameco though it is a uranium miner. Oklo ranks as early-stage and pre-revenue versus these more established nuclear players.

## 3. Longterm

3.1. **Stability?:** Oklo is an early-stage microreactor developer with no revenue, cumulative net losses of about $74.5M, and negative operating/free cash flow, indicating limited financial stability so far. Market risk is elevated, with high volatility (ATR ~13% of price) and a recent downtrend. While DOE-related progress on its Aurora Fuel Fabrication Facility and analyst coverage were reported, it has not yet demonstrated cash-generating operations. It is therefore not yet a stable, cash-flowing utility.

3.2. **Innovating?:** Innovating and pursuing growth: it is developing the Aurora liquid metal fast reactor (up to 15 MWe) and used nuclear fuel recycling. A recent DOE greenlight for its Aurora Fuel Fabrication Facility underscores ongoing development progress.
